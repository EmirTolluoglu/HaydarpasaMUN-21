SPECPOL

Introduction

X

  SPECPOL stands for "The Special Political and Decolonization Committee" , the fourth committee of the General Assembly of the United Nations. As it can be understood by its name, SPECPOL’s main goal is the debate upon decolonization-related agendas and dealing with current political issues that can have a global impact. SPECPOL furthermore addresses sensitive political issues from peacekeeping, peaceful use of outer space, refugees, UN peacekeeping, and human rights. With the developed technology and current research that is actively being carried out, the issue of space travel and sovereignty has become one of the major subject matters. Thus, the future of recent advancements made in space travel and the issue of space colonialism holds a crucial spot to be discussed following the Space 2030 Agenda. The second agenda item for this committee is related to the question of the United States Virgin Islands. The disagreement upon the right of self-government has been a major political issue since the mid-20th century. The government of the U.S and U.S Virgin Islands have been debating upon the administrative and political status quo of the island and its citizens. It is expected of delegates to find solutions regarding this question.

Agenda Item 1:  Questioning the future of recent advancements made in space travel and colonization in relation to the Space 2030 Agenda


Agenda Item 2:  Question of United States Virgin Islands
