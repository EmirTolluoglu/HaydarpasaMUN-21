United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR)

Introduction

X

  The United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees was established by the General Assembly in 1950 after the Second World War and during the refugee waves that it caused. UNHCR helped millions of Europeans who lost their homes or fled them for safety get back to their homeland safely. The UNHCR operates according to the 1951 Refugee Convention and it is based in Geneva Switzerland. Today UNHCR protects and aids twenty million refugees worldwide.
With the 2022 edition of HaydarpasaMUN, we welcome all the delegates in UNHCR to further discuss the effects the Covid-19 pandemic had on the refugee waves and how to ensure the safety of the refugees, and how to delicately handle the Ukrainian refugee crisis caused by the invasion of Ukraine by the Russian Federation. We invite all delegates to debate and come up with a sufficient resolution.
