United Nations Security Council

Introduction

X

  With supreme powers over the other United Nations main organs, the Security Council remains as one of the six principal organs of the UN. It has 15 members, 5 of them being permanent members, the remaining 10 non-permanent members are elected on a regional basis to serve for two years. The Security Council is responsible for investigating aggressive actions which can harm international peace and security. Apart from protecting security and peace, the Security Council recommends to the General Assembly the appointment of the Secretary-General and together with the assembly, they elect the judges of the International Court Of Justice.
In this annual session of HaydarpasaMUN, the swift and distinct decision-making mechanism of UNSC focuses on resolving the Question of Regional Agencies’ Involvement in Interstate Relations and situations in Sudan and South Sudan. Both items lead the delegates to discuss the principles of International Relations while also granting them the right to inspect the questions concerning peace and security. Will the politically diverse delegates of the house accomplish to find a common ground upon these across-the-board issues?
